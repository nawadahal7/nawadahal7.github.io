Nawa Dahal

Applied Mathematics Student
San José State University

My research interests lie in nonlinear partial differential equations, numerical analysis, and scientific computing, particularly in problems where physical modeling, mathematical analysis, and computation interact. I am interested in the analysis of numerical methods, including questions of convergence, stability, conditioning, and approximation, as well as their applications to PDE models arising in physics and other applied sciences.

Research

  • Numerical Investigation of the Poisson–Boltzmann Equation
    Honors thesis involving nonlinear PDEs, finite-difference methods, fixed-point iterations, and semiconductor modeling.
  • A Matrix-Based Perspective on Multi-Level Richardson Extrapolation
    Research on numerical integration, truncation error reduction, and finite-precision effects.
  • Modeling and Numerical Simulation of Solar Cells
    Ongoing research extending semiconductor simulations to photovoltaic devices.
