Notes & Thoughts

This page is a place for informal mathematical notes, research reflections, and questions I keep coming back to. Some of these notes come from coursework, some from research, and some from random questions that kept bothering me until I had to write them down.

These notes are not polished papers. They are closer to a mathematical notebook: partly exposition, partly reflection, and partly a record of how my thinking is changing over time.

A Semester Thinking About Dynamical Systems

During Fall 2025, I took my first course in Dynamical Systems. Up to that point, most of my experience with differential equations came from lower-division courses, where the emphasis was largely on finding solutions explicitly solutions. Like, given an equation, the goal was to solve it. The course emphasized qualitative behavior: fixed points, stability, phase portraits, bifurcations, and the overall structure of the solution space. One of the biggest conceptual shifts for me in this course was realizing that solving a differential equation is not always the most interesting question. During the first few weeks, I often found myself wanting to separate variables, find a closed-form solution, or compute an exact answer. Letting go of that instinct was really difficult. Over time, I began to appreciate a different perspective. Rather than asking “Can I solve this equation?”, I started asking questions such as “What do all solutions do?”, “Which qualitative behaviors are possible?”, and “How does the geometry of the system determine its dynamics?” Looking back, that transition from computation to qualitative reasoning was probably the most influential part of the course for me. It changed the way I think about differential equations and played an important role in my growing interest in dynamical systems, PDEs, and applied mathematics more broadly. The notes below grew out of three course reflections written throughout the semester. In those reflections, students were asked to discuss strengths, weaknesses, plans for improvement, and an application of their choice related to the material. The discussions presented here are not the original submissions. Instead, they are revised and expanded versions that preserve the main ideas, applications and questions that stayed with me even after the course ended.
