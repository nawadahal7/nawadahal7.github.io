Research

My research interests lie in numerical analysis, nonlinear partial differential equations, and scientific computing. I am particularly interested in problems where physical modeling, mathematical analysis, and computation interact.

A Matrix-Based Perspective on Multi-Level Richardson Extrapolation

This project investigates multi-level Richardson extrapolation methods for numerical integration. The work focuses on error reduction, finite-precision effects, conditioning, and numerical stability. A manuscript based on this work was submitted to SIAM Undergraduate Research Online (SIURO).

Numerical Investigation of the Poisson–Boltzmann Equation

This honors thesis explores finite-difference discretizations and iterative methods for nonlinear Poisson–Boltzmann equations arising in semiconductor modeling. The project includes the analysis of fixed-point, Newton, and quasi-linearization methods, together with MATLAB implementations and numerical experiments.

Modeling and Numerical Simulation of Solar Cells

This ongoing project extends previous semiconductor drift–diffusion–Poisson modeling work to photovoltaic and solar-cell applications. Current interests include photogeneration, carrier transport, recombination mechanisms, current–voltage characteristics, power generation, fill factor, and device efficiency.
