Research

My research interests lie in numerical analysis, nonlinear partial differential equations, and scientific computing, particularly in problems where physical modeling, mathematical analysis, and computation interact.

A Matrix-Based Perspective on Multi-Level Richardson Extrapolation

This project investigates multi-level Richardson extrapolation methods for numerical integration. The work focuses on error reduction, finite-precision effects, conditioning, and numerical stability.

A manuscript based on this work was submitted to SIAM Undergraduate Research Online (SIURO).

Numerical Investigation of the Poisson–Boltzmann Equation

This honors thesis investigates finite-difference discretizations and iterative methods for nonlinear Poisson–Boltzmann equations arising in semiconductor modeling. The project includes mathematical analysis, MATLAB implementation, and numerical experiments.

One outcome of this project was a research poster presented at San José State University Research Day. Research Poster (PDF)

The honors thesis has been completed. Portions of the work continue to evolve into future projects and are therefore not currently posted online.

Modeling and Numerical Simulation of Solar Cells

This ongoing project extends previous semiconductor modeling work toward photovoltaic and solar-cell applications, including photogeneration, carrier transport, recombination, current-voltage characteristics, power generation, fill factor, and device efficiency.

More projects will be added here as they develop.
